Guru Smt. Daya Ravi

Smt. Daya Ravi founded the Natraj School of Indian Dance in 1996, where she teaches Bharatanatyam theory and practice in Boyds, Maryland. Introduced to this Divine art form by H. H. Swami Haridhos Giri, she learned the Pandanallur style from Kalaimamani Smt. Krishnakumari Narendran and the Kanchipuram Ellappa style from Acharya Guru Sri. Udupi Lakshminarayanan, blending both styles in her choreographies. Smt. Daya is certified in Indian Music and Dance theory and practice and conduct Yoga and Theory workshops for her students. She has been performing at prestigious venues worldwide since childhood. Notably, she was honored by the late President Jayawardhana of Sri Lanka for her Shiva Thandavam performance and in 1980, received the Best Child Artist award from the state of Tamil Nadu for her role in the movie "Purandaradasar." Smt. Daya is actively involved in promoting dance to mainstream audiences and has performed at festivals and events such as FETNA, IDEA, Smithsonian Sackler Art Gallery, and the National Cherry Blossom Festival, Wolf trap, World Cultural Festivals. She has also served on the judging panel for various competitions across the DMV area. Smt. Daya believes in the purpose of art and donates proceeds from NSID's annual day to charities. She has organized charitable programs and workshops and graduated over 35 students. She emphasizes the Guru-Shisya relationship and instills devotion and cultural roots in her students. Smt. Daya Ravi has received recognition and awards for her teaching and service. She has taught dance to special needs kids at a public school and incorporates yoga and breathing techniques in her teaching. She expresses gratitude to her family for their support and considers dance a moving meditation that brings harmony to her body, mind, spirit, and soul.
